The event brought together aspiring entrepreneurs and innovation enthusiasts from international programs across the university. The highlight was meeting Dr. Montri Meeseepong, founder of Quvit Bio, a talented nano-engineering expert who won 2nd Runner Up at Siriraj & MIT Hacking Medicine and is part of the NVIDIA Inception program, sharing tips on building a biotech startup. The three-hour event was packed with valuable content, including club introduction, the story of Quvit Bio's establishment, and networking activities. This collaboration demonstrates the International College of Digital Innovation's commitment to nurturing new entrepreneurs, combining both theory and real-world experience to provide students with comprehensive learning opportunities. This partnership between DiNovate Club and builds CMU marks an important first step in building a strong startup ecosystem, ready to transform students' innovative ideas into real businesses.
