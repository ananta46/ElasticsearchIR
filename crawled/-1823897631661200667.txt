September 2, 2022, via Zoom Meeting platform and Nurse KC Moodle Platform. Sixty health personnel from 18 countries registered for the course, including Bhutan, Burkina Faso, Costa Rica, Guinea, Indonesia, Iraq, Jordan, Kazakhstan, Maldivian, Mozambique, Myanmar, Nepal, Philippines, Saint Kitts and Nevis, Sri Lanka, Uganda, Vietnam, Yemen. Miss Hataichanok Siriwadhanakul, Human Resources Development Cooperation Division, Thailand International Cooperation Agency (TICA), delivered a welcome speech, and Asst.  Prof. Dr.Thanee Kaewthummanukul, Ph.D., RN Dean, Faculty of Nursing, Chiang Mai University, gave the opening address to the participants. Training Objectives for the Course: 1. Understand the health care system and occupational health and safety system and policy in Thailand 2. Explain fundamental principles of occupational health and safety 3. Understand basic knowledge of occupational health and safety 4. Describe the role and function of public health personnel in providing occupational health and safety services 5. Perform a workplace walk-through survey to assess and identify risks related to work 6. Understand principles of occupational health surveillance and screening 7.
